Understanding Histograms

A histogram is a graphical representation of the distribution of data. It displays the frequencies or counts of data points falling within specified intervals or "bins" along a continuous range.

Interpreting a Histogram:
X-axis: Represents the range of values of the variable being measured or observed.
Y-axis: Displays the frequency or count of data points falling within each bin on the x-axis.

Studying a Histogram:
Shape: Analyze the shape of the histogram to understand the distribution pattern.
Center: Identify the central tendency (mean, median, mode) from the peak or center of the histogram.
Spread: Determine the spread or variability of the data from the width of the distribution.
Outliers: Look for any outliers or unusual data points that fall far from the main distribution.
