The mean, also known as the average, is a measure of central tendency in a set of numerical data. It is calculated by adding up all the values in the dataset and then dividing the sum by the total number of values.

Another measure of central tendency is median, which represents the middle value of a dataset when it is sorted in ascending or descending order. If the dataset has an odd number of values, the median is the middle value. If the dataset has an even number of values, the median is the average of the two middle values.
