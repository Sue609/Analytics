Understanding Box Plots

A box plot, also known as a box-and-whisker plot, is a graphical method to display the distribution of a dataset. It summarizes the central tendency, dispersion, and skewness of the data.

Interpreting a Box Plot:
The box represents the interquartile range (IQR), which contains the middle 50% of the data.
The whiskers extend from the box and represent the range of the data.
Outliers are represented as individual points beyond the whiskers.
The line inside the box represents the median of the data.
